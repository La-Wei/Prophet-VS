3@ CHANNEL SAMPLE & HOLD

Preliminary, January 1987

Description

The CEM 5539 is a 38 channel multiplexed Sample and Hold intended

for generating multiple system control voltages or moderate speed

multiplexed digital audio.

Containing an auto zeroing op amp,

analog multiplexer, 5 bit decoder, and 38 individual Sample and

Holds capable of settling to 12 bits in under 2 uS, the 5530

can sample a voltage from a DAC into any one of its 38 outputs

with less than 2 mV error from -5 to +5 volts.

Operation of the 5530 is straightforward: Bringing INH high puts

all channels in the hold mode and causes the input op amp to auto

zero.

The DAC data and channel address are then changed, and

while the DAC and decoder are settling, input offset is being

nulled out.

Ease of

CMOS logic inputs which are TTL compatible regardless of

With all hold capacitors on-chip and requiring no external

components, the CEM 5530 makes interface between microprocessor

and voltage controlled devices exceptionally simple and

economical.
